\section{Updated Notes B-I}
%% --
For the abstract characterization of spaces of continuous functions as commutative \CA-algebras, \ie the Gelfand-Naimark theorem, see \mycite[Chapter I-3]{zbmath01692441}. 
For concepts such as ideals, their connections with closed sets, and the representation of lattice or algebra homomorphisms, we refer to \mycite{zbmath03357434}. 
The various types of positive operators on these algebras are discussed in \mycite[Chapter 4]{zbmath06423122}. 
Semigroups on spaces of continuous functions generated by elliptic
operators in non-divergence form are treated in the monograph
\mycite{zbmath00732330}.
