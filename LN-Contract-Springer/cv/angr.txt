**Annette Räbiger (née Grabosch)** received her Ph.D. in Mathematics
from the University of Tübingen in 1985 with the dissertation *Die
Lösungshalbgruppe abstrakter retardierter Gleichungen* under the
supervision of Rainer Nagel. She held visiting positions at Georgetown
University (Washington, D.C.) and at the Centre for Mathematics and
Computer Science (CWI, Amsterdam) in the Department of Mathematical
Biology. Subsequently, she worked for several years in the Mathematical
Biology group at the University of Tübingen, collaborating with research
teams in Tübingen, Amsterdam, and Pau (France) on topics at the
interface of functional analysis and applications in biology. Since
1995, Annette Räbiger has focused on her family and pursued independent
work in mathematics education, psychology, and pedagogy.
