**Frank Neubrander** is Alumni Professor of Mathematics and Executive
Director and Chair of the Gordon A. Cain Center for STEM Literacy at
Louisiana State University. He received his Ph.D. in Mathematics under
the supervision of Rainer Nagel from the University of Tübingen in 1984
and held visiting positions at Georgetown University from 1984 to 1988
before joining LSU in 1989. His research interests include evolution
equations, Laplace transform methods, and mathematics education. He is a
coauthor of *One-Parameter Semigroups of Positive Operators* (Lecture
Notes in Mathematics 1184, Springer 1986) and *Vector-Valued Laplace
Transforms and Cauchy Problems* (Birkhäuser 2011), and coeditor of
*Functional Analysis and Evolution Equations: The Günter Lumer Volume*
(Birkhäuser 2008). At LSU he has supervised numerous graduate students
and led STEM education initiatives supported by the National Science
Foundation and the U.S. Department of Education.
