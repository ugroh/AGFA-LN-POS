**Günther Greiner** is Professor Emeritus of Computer Science at the
University of Erlangen--Nürnberg. He studied physics and mathematics at
the University of Tübingen, where he also earned his Ph.D. and completed
his habilitation in mathematics. After serving as Lecturer and later
Assistant Professor (Hochschuldozent) in the Department of Mathematics
at Tübingen, he developed a growing interest in computer science,
focusing on computer graphics and visual computing. In 1998, he was
appointed Chair of Computer Science at the School of Engineering of the
University of Erlangen--Nürnberg. Over the course of his career, he held
several long-term research appointments abroad, including at the
University of California, Berkeley; the Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica
(CWI) in Amsterdam; the Institute for Mathematics and Its Applications
(IMA) in Minnesota; and the University of Zurich. He made significant
contributions to *One-Parameter Semigroups of Positive Operators* and
served as editor and co-author of the monograph *Principles of 3D Image
Analysis and Synthesis* (2nd printing, 2002).
