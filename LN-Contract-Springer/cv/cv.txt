**Wolfgang Arendt** is Professor Emeritus of Mathematics at Ulm
University. He studied mathematics and physics in Berlin, Nice, and
Tübingen, where he earned his doctorate under H. H. Schaefer (1979) and
completed his habilitation (1985). From 1987 to 1995 he was Professor at
the Université de Franche-Comté in Besançon before joining Ulm
University as Chair of Applied Analysis (1995--2018). His research
focuses on functional analysis and the theory of partial differential
equations. He is coauthor of *One-Parameter Semigroups of Positive
Operators* (Springer LNM 1184, 1986), *Vector-Valued Laplace Transforms
and Cauchy Problems* (Birkhäuser 2001), and *Partial Differential
Equations: An Introduction to Analytical and Numerical Methods*
(Springer 2018, with K. Urban), and coeditor (with W. P. Schleich) of
*Mathematical Analysis of Evolution, Information, and Complexity* (Wiley
2009). He has supervised thirty-four doctoral students, serves as
Editor-in-Chief of the *Journal of Evolution Equations*, and co-founded
the International Internet Seminar on Evolution Equations. His numerous
research stays include visits to Berkeley, Oxford, Zurich, Sydney,
Auckland, Stanford, and Paris.

**Günther Greiner** is Professor Emeritus of Computer Science at the
University of Erlangen--Nürnberg. He studied physics and mathematics at
the University of Tübingen, where he also earned his Ph.D. and completed
his habilitation in mathematics. After serving as Lecturer and later
Assistant Professor (Hochschuldozent) in the Department of Mathematics
at Tübingen, he developed a growing interest in computer science,
focusing on computer graphics and visual computing. In 1998, he was
appointed Chair of Computer Science at the School of Engineering of the
University of Erlangen--Nürnberg. Over the course of his career, he held
several long-term research appointments abroad, including at the
University of California, Berkeley; the Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica
(CWI) in Amsterdam; the Institute for Mathematics and Its Applications
(IMA) in Minnesota; and the University of Zurich. He made significant
contributions to *One-Parameter Semigroups of Positive Operators* and
served as editor and co-author of the monograph *Principles of 3D Image
Analysis and Synthesis* (2nd printing, 2002).

**Ulrich Groh** has been affiliated with the Mathematical Institute of
the University of Tübingen since 2009. He studied mathematics and
computer science at Tübingen in the 1970s and earned his Ph.D. in 1979
under H. H. Schaefer. His research focuses on the spectral theory of
positive operators on C\*- and W\*-algebras. After a one-year research
fellowship at Hokkaido University, supported by the Alexander von
Humboldt Foundation and the Japan Society for the Promotion of Science,
he completed his habilitation in 1984 and obtained the *venia legendi*
at Tübingen. He is a coauthor of *One-Parameter Semigroups of Positive
Operators* (Lecture Notes in Mathematics 1184, Springer 1986), which
includes results from his habilitation thesis. In 1985, he joined IBM
Germany, where he ultimately became head of the *Lehre und Forschung*
(Education and Research) division, contributing to national digital
archival projects and university IT initiatives. From 2007 to 2011, he
served on the Board of Trustees of the Stuttgart Technical University of
Applied Sciences. In 2012, the University of Tübingen awarded him the
title of Adjunct Professor (apl. Prof.).

**Ulrich Moustakas** (University of Tübingen) received the Dr. rer. nat.
in Mathematics in 1985 with the dissertation *Majorisierung und
Spektraleigenschaften positiver Operatoren auf Banachverbänden*
(*Majorization and Spectral Properties of Positive Operators on Banach
Lattices*). Soon thereafter he turned to theology and later habilitated
in Systematic Theology at the University of Tübingen (Privatdozent). His
theological publications include *Theologie im Kontext von
Wissenschaftstheorie und Hermeneutik* (Verlag Dr. Kovač, 2017).

**Rainer Nagel** is Professor Emeritus of Mathematics at the University
of Tübingen and a leading authority in functional analysis and operator
semigroup theory. A doctoral student of H. H. Schaefer, he became
internationally recognized for his contributions to one-parameter
semigroups and positivity. He served as editor and coordinating
co-author of *One-Parameter Semigroups of Positive Operators* (Springer
LNM 1184, 1986), a landmark monograph now appearing in its second
edition. His subsequent books include *One-Parameter Semigroups for
Linear Evolution Equations* (with K.-J. Engel, GTM 194, 2000), *A Short
Course on Operator Semigroups* (2006), and *Operator Theoretic Aspects
of Ergodic Theory* (GTM 272, 2015). Professor Nagel co-founded the
*Journal of Evolution Equations*, held editorial leadership at
*Semigroup Forum*, and initiated the *International Internet Seminar on
Evolution Equations* (since 1998). He also founded the *Romseminare* in
1993, an innovative interdisciplinary seminar format in which
mathematics students explore connections between mathematics and other
sciences, the arts, and society---an initiative that continues
successfully to this day.

**Frank Neubrander** is Alumni Professor of Mathematics and Executive
Director and Chair of the Gordon A. Cain Center for STEM Literacy at
Louisiana State University. He received his Ph.D. in Mathematics under
the supervision of Rainer Nagel from the University of Tübingen in 1984
and held visiting positions at Georgetown University from 1984 to 1988
before joining LSU in 1989. His research interests include evolution
equations, Laplace transform methods, and mathematics education. He is a
coauthor of *One-Parameter Semigroups of Positive Operators* (Lecture
Notes in Mathematics 1184, Springer 1986) and *Vector-Valued Laplace
Transforms and Cauchy Problems* (Birkhäuser 2011), and coeditor of
*Functional Analysis and Evolution Equations: The Günter Lumer Volume*
(Birkhäuser 2008). At LSU he has supervised numerous graduate students
and led STEM education initiatives supported by the National Science
Foundation and the U.S. Department of Education.

**Annette Räbiger (née Grabosch)** received her Ph.D. in Mathematics
from the University of Tübingen in 1985 with the dissertation *Die
Lösungshalbgruppe abstrakter retardierter Gleichungen* under the
supervision of Rainer Nagel. She held visiting positions at Georgetown
University (Washington, D.C.) and at the Centre for Mathematics and
Computer Science (CWI, Amsterdam) in the Department of Mathematical
Biology. Subsequently, she worked for several years in the Mathematical
Biology group at the University of Tübingen, collaborating with research
teams in Tübingen, Amsterdam, and Pau (France) on topics at the
interface of functional analysis and applications in biology. Since
1995, Annette Räbiger has focused on her family and pursued independent
work in mathematics education, psychology, and pedagogy.
