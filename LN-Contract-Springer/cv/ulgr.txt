**Ulrich Groh** has been affiliated with the Mathematical Institute of
the University of Tübingen since 2009. He studied mathematics and
computer science at Tübingen in the 1970s and earned his Ph.D. in 1979
under H. H. Schaefer. His research focuses on the spectral theory of
positive operators on C\*- and W\*-algebras. After a one-year research
fellowship at Hokkaido University, supported by the Alexander von
Humboldt Foundation and the Japan Society for the Promotion of Science,
he completed his habilitation in 1984 and obtained the *venia legendi*
at Tübingen. He is a coauthor of *One-Parameter Semigroups of Positive
Operators* (Lecture Notes in Mathematics 1184, Springer 1986), which
includes results from his habilitation thesis. In 1985, he joined IBM
Germany, where he ultimately became head of the *Lehre und Forschung*
(Education and Research) division, contributing to national digital
archival projects and university IT initiatives. From 2007 to 2011, he
served on the Board of Trustees of the Stuttgart Technical University of
Applied Sciences. In 2012, the University of Tübingen awarded him the
title of Adjunct Professor (apl. Prof.).
